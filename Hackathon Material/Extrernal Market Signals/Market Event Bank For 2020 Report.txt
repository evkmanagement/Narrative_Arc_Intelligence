# Market Event Bank — EVForward Period 1 (Jan–Sep 2019, U.S. Light-Duty BEVs/PHEVs)

## SIG-POL-2019-001

**Date**  
2019-01-01 (Tesla phase-out begins) / 2019-04-01 (GM phase-out begins)

**Headline**  
Federal EV tax credit phase-outs begin for early market leaders

**Category**  
Policy

**Signal Class**  
Structural

**Signal Vector**  
Policy

**Signal Direction**  
Reduction

**Effective Window**  
- Start: 2019-01-01 (Tesla reduced to $3,750)  
- Midpoint: 2019-07-01 (Tesla reduced to $1,875)  
- GM start: 2019-04-01 (GM reduced to 50%)  
- End: 2019-09-30 (GM reduced to $1,875 for next window; Tesla credit ends 2019-12-31)[web:129][web:127][web:283][web:6]

**Observed Change**  
The federal plug‑in EV tax credit for Tesla vehicles was reduced from $7,500 to $3,750 on January 1, 2019 and to $1,875 on July 1, 2019 after Tesla exceeded 200,000 qualifying EV sales.[web:129][web:11] The federal EV tax credit for GM vehicles began phasing down on April 1, 2019, with eligible models such as the Chevrolet Bolt EV receiving $3,750 instead of $7,500 through September 30, 2019 before dropping to $1,875 for the next phase.[web:127][web:286][web:6]

**Evidence Themes**  
Affordability, Financing, Incentives, Policy, Model Availability

**Primary Source**  
IRS notices on Tesla and GM Section 30D phase-outs, summarized by InsideEVs and Drive Electric MN[web:129][web:127][web:11][web:6]

**Evidence Strength**  
High — IRS primary documents and Congressional Research Service note, corroborated by major EV outlets[web:127][web:283][web:11]

**Verification Status**  
Verified

**Date**

**Headline**  
U.S. plug-in vehicle sales grow but remain a small share of new light-duty sales

**Category**  
Economy

**Signal Class**  
Market

**Signal Vector**  
Economy

**Signal Direction**  
Increase (market share and cumulative sales)

**Effective Window**  
- Start: 2019-03-31 (EEI Q1 2019 snapshot; EV Volumes H1 2019 update)  
- End: 2019-09-30 (EVForward Jan–Sep window within the 2019 sales context)[web:280][web:279][web:285][web:284]

**Observed Change**  
EEI reported that EV market share grew to 1.8% of U.S. new car sales in March 2019, up from 1.6% a year earlier, with about 61,000 EVs sold in Q1 2019 and cumulative EVs on U.S. roads exceeding 1.18 million as of March 31, 2019.[web:280] EV Volumes and DOE data indicated that plug‑ins accounted for around 1.8–1.9% of light‑vehicle sales in 2019 and about 1.8% of sales in the first half of 2019.[web:279][web:285][web:284]

Awareness, Market Penetration, Social Proof (Number of Owners), Competition, Product Availability
