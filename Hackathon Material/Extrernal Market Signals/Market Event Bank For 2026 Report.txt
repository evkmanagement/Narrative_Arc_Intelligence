## SIG-INFRA-2024-012

**Date**  
2024-12-31 (Q4 2024 NEVI snapshot; reported in Q4 2024 NEVI update)

**Headline**  
NEVI network reaches 31 stations and 126 fast-charging ports as U.S. public charging passes 200,000 ports

**Category**  
Infrastructure

**Signal Class**  
Structural

**Signal Vector**  
Infrastructure

**Signal Direction**  
Expansion

**Effective Window**  
- Start: 2024-12-31 (status at end of 2024)  
- End: 2025-11-30 (baseline infrastructure level entering and during this EVForward window)[web:435][web:432]

**Observed Change**  
The Joint Office of Energy and Transportation’s Q4 2024 NEVI update reported that there were 126 public fast‑charging ports operating across 31 NEVI stations in nine U.S. states, an 83% increase in open NEVI ports compared with the prior quarter.[web:435] The same update noted that the total U.S. public charging network had grown to nearly 204,000 ports by late 2024, roughly double the number of public chargers at the end of 2020, and that about 59.1% of the most heavily trafficked highway corridors now had fast chargers at least every 50 miles.[web:435][web:432]

**Evidence Themes**  
Infrastructure Deployment, Public Fast Charging, Charging Accessibility, Federal Funding Implementation, Highway Corridors

**Primary Source**

## SIG-POL-2025-014

**Date**  
2025-07-04

**Headline**  
“One Big Beautiful Bill Act” signed, ending federal EV tax credits for new and used vehicles after September 30, 2025

**Category**  
Policy

**Signal Class**  
Structural

**Signal Vector**  
Policy

**Signal Direction**  
Policy Change

**Effective Window**  
- Start: 2025-07-04 (law signed)  
- End: 2025-09-30 (last date to enter qualifying contracts for new/used EV tax credits)[web:446][web:449][web:451][web:450][web:455]

**Observed Change**  
On July 4, 2025, President Trump signed the “One Big Beautiful Bill Act,” a tax and spending package that terminates the federal clean vehicle tax credits for new and used EVs earlier than previously scheduled, setting a cutoff for acquisitions after September 30, 2025.[web:446][web:449][web:455] The law ends the $7,500 tax credit for qualifying new EV purchases and leases and the $4,000 credit for qualifying used EVs as of that date, replacing the Inflation Reduction Act’s original expiration date of December 31, 2032.[web:449][web:451][web:450]

**Evidence Themes**  
Policy, Incentives, Affordability, Eligibility Rules, Purchase Timing

**Primary Source**  
CNBC, Reuters, and electrek reporting on the One Big Beautiful Bill Act’s EV tax credit provisions, along with tax and policy explainers referencing the September 30, 2025 cutoff[web:446][web:451][web:455][web:450]

**Evidence Strength**  
High — national business media and specialized tax/policy resources describing enacted legislation[web:446][web:451]

## SIG-POL-2025-015

**Date**  
2025-09-30

**Headline**  
Federal new and used clean vehicle tax credits expire for vehicles purchased after September 30, 2025

**Category**  
Policy

**Signal Class**  
Structural

**Signal Vector**  
Policy

**Signal Direction**  
Reduction

**Effective Window**  
- Start: 2025-09-30 (last day to enter a binding contract and qualify)  
- End: 2025-11-30 (initial post‑credit environment within this EVForward window)[web:454][web:451][web:452][web:448][web:453]

**Observed Change**  
Following enactment of the One Big Beautiful Bill Act, federal clean vehicle credits worth up to $7,500 for new EVs and $4,000 for used EVs ceased to be available for vehicles purchased or leased after September 30, 2025, with eligibility limited to buyers who had a binding contract and payment in place by that date.[web:454][web:451][web:452] Consumer and tax guidance clarified that purchases completed after the cutoff would no longer qualify for the Section 30D new clean vehicle credit or the used EV credit despite earlier expectations of availability through 2032 under the Inflation Reduction Act.[web:448][web:453][web:450]

**Evidence Themes**  
Policy, Incentives, Affordability, Purchase Timing, Dealer Practices

**Primary Source**  
NPR coverage of the September 30, 2025 EV tax credit deadline, electrek’s summary of the official end of the credits, and tax guidance describing the cutoff and binding contract rules[web:454][web:451][web:452][web:450]

**Evidence Strength**  
High — widely reported policy change with consistent treatment across media and tax resources[web:454][web:451]
