**Date**  
2023-12-13

**Headline**  
First NEVI-funded DC fast-charging station opens in London, Ohio

**Category**  
Infrastructure

**Signal Class**  
Structural

**Signal Vector**  
Infrastructure

**Signal Direction**  
Expansion

**Effective Window**  
- Start: 2023-12-13 (station opens to public)  
- End: 2024-11-30 (station remains in service throughout this EVForward window)[web:400][web:392]

**Observed Change**  
The Ohio Department of Transportation and Pilot Flying J opened a DC fast‑charging station in London, Ohio on December 13, 2023 that state officials identified as the first site built using National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) Formula Program funds.[web:400][web:392] The station includes 350 kW DC fast chargers that can deliver up to 175 kW per vehicle when both sides of a pylon are in use and is located along an interstate corridor west of Columbus.[web:400]

Infrastructure Deployment, Public Fast Charging, Federal Funding Implementation, Highway Corridors, Charging Accessibility

**Primary Source**

**Category**  
Economy

**Signal Class**

**Signal Vector**

**Signal Direction**

**Effective Window**  
- Start: 2010-12-01 (start of mass-market plug-in sales)

**Observed Change**  
DOE’s Transportation Fact of the Week #1326 reported that cumulative U.S. sales of new light‑duty plug‑in electric vehicles (battery electric and plug‑in hybrid combined) reached 4.7 million units by the end of 2023.[web:422] The fact sheet noted that it took about eight years to reach the first 1 million plug‑in sales, but only about nine months to grow cumulative sales from 4 million to 4.7 million.[web:422]

## SIG-ECO-2024-002

**Date**  
2024-01-01 – 2024-03-31 (Q1 2024)

**Headline**  
EV and hybrid share of U.S. light-duty sales declines slightly in Q1 2024 as BEV share slips

**Category**  
Economy

**Signal Class**  
Market

**Signal Vector**  
Economy

**Signal Direction**  
Decrease (share, not absolute volume)

**Effective Window**  
- Start: 2024-01-01  
- End: 2024-03-31 (first quarter 2024 sales context)[web:410][web:404]

**Observed Change**  
The U.S. Energy Information Administration, citing Wards Intelligence estimates, reported that hybrid, plug‑in hybrid, and battery electric vehicles collectively accounted for 18.0% of new light‑duty vehicle sales in Q1 2024, down from 18.8% in Q4 2023, with BEV share falling from 8.1% to 7.0% over the same period.[web:410] Independent analysis summarizing EIA data similarly noted that combined EV and hybrid share slipped in Q1 2024 even though EV sales volumes were about 7% higher than in Q1 2023.[web:404]

**Evidence Themes**  
Market Penetration, Competition (EV vs ICE), Awareness, Operating Environment

**Primary Source**  
EIA Today in Energy article on EV and hybrid market share in Q1 2024, supplemented by Electricity Forum summary of the same trend[web:410][web:404]

**Evidence Strength**  
High — federal statistics and consistent secondary analysis[web:410]

**Category**

**Effective Window**  
- Start: 2024-03-20 (final rule signed)  
- End: 2032-12-31 (phase-in period for MY 2027–2032 standards)[web:417][web:430][web:424]

**Observed Change**  
On March 20, 2024, EPA finalized multi‑pollutant emissions standards for model years 2027–2032 light‑ and medium‑duty vehicles, tightening fleet‑average greenhouse gas and criteria pollutant limits compared with existing 2023–2026 standards.[web:417][web:430] EPA and subsequent technical summaries indicated that the performance‑based rule is consistent with compliance scenarios that could lead to plug‑in electric vehicles (battery electric plus plug‑in hybrids) reaching around 53% of new light‑duty sales by 2030 and about 68% by 2032.[web:430][web:424]

## SIG-SUP-2023-005

**Date**  
2023-12-10 (supplier memo reported) / 2024-01-18–19 (public confirmation)

**Headline**  
Ford plans to cut F-150 Lightning production roughly in half for 2024

**Category**  
Supply Chain

**Signal Class**  
Market

**Signal Vector**  
Supply

**Signal Direction**  
Reduction

**Effective Window**  
- Start: 2023-12-10 (production plan memo to suppliers reported)  
- Midpoint: 2024-04-01 (one-shift schedule at Rouge EV Center takes effect)  
- End: 2024-11-30 (reduced production rate during this EVForward window)[web:411][web:413][web:414][web:409]

**Observed Change**  
In December 2023, reporting based on an internal Ford memo indicated the company planned to cut production of the F‑150 Lightning electric pickup from a previously expected 3,200 units per week to about 1,600 units per week in 2024, a reduction of roughly 50%.[web:411][web:414] In January 2024, Ford publicly confirmed it would scale back F‑150 Lightning production to one shift at the Rouge Electric Vehicle Center starting April 1, 2024, affecting about 1,400 workers and aligning output with “changing market demand.”[web:413][web:409]

**Evidence Themes**  
Production Capacity, Model Availability, Supply Chain Resilience, Pricing and Profitability
