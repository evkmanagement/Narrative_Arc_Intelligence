## SIG-POL-2020-001

**Date**  
2020-09-23

**Headline**  
California sets 2035 goal for 100% zero-emission new light-duty vehicle sales

**Category**  
Policy

**Signal Class**  
Structural

**Signal Vector**  
Policy

**Signal Direction**  
Policy Change

**Effective Window**  
- Start: 2020-09-23 (Executive Order issued)  
- End: Still Active (target year 2035; implementing regulations under development)[web:290][web:292][web:295]

**Observed Change**  
California Governor Gavin Newsom issued Executive Order N‑79‑20 setting a goal that by 2035, 100% of in‑state sales of new passenger cars and trucks will be zero‑emission vehicles.[web:290][web:292][web:295] The order directed the California Air Resources Board to develop regulations to implement the 2035 sales requirement and called for expansion of ZEV fueling and charging infrastructure statewide.[web:290][web:292][web:298]

**Evidence Themes**  
Policy, Incentives, Model Availability, Infrastructure Planning, Long-Term Targets

**Primary Source**

**Category**  
Economy

**Signal Class**

**Signal Vector**

**Signal Direction**  
Increase (EV share vs total market)

**Effective Window**  
- Start: 2020-01-01

**Observed Change**  
Analyses based on EV Volumes data estimated that U.S. plug‑in vehicle sales totaled roughly 328,000 units in 2020, about 4% higher than 2019 while the overall light‑duty auto market declined by about 15%.[web:307] These estimates placed 2020 U.S. plug‑in market share in the 2–3% range of total light‑duty vehicle sales.[web:307]

Market Penetration, Social Proof, Competition (EV vs ICE), Awareness
