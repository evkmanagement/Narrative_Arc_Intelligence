## SIG-POL-2023-001

**Date**  
2023-03-30 / 2023-04-18 (effective date for requirements)

**Headline**  
Treasury issues proposed guidance implementing IRA critical mineral and battery component rules for EV tax credits

**Category**  
Policy

**Signal Class**  
Structural

**Signal Vector**  
Policy

**Signal Direction**  
Policy Change

**Effective Window**  
- Start: 2023-04-18 (critical mineral and battery component rules apply to vehicles placed in service on or after this date)  
- End: 2032-12-31 (statutory end of clean vehicle credit under IRA)[web:376][web:382][web:380]

**Observed Change**  
On March 30, 2023, the U.S. Treasury and IRS released proposed guidance on new clean vehicle tax credit provisions, detailing how manufacturers must calculate compliance with Inflation Reduction Act critical mineral and battery component sourcing requirements.[web:376][web:382] Treasury stated that beginning with vehicles placed in service on or after April 18, 2023, the $7,500 clean vehicle credit is split into a $3,750 portion for meeting escalating critical mineral sourcing thresholds and a $3,750 portion for meeting escalating North American battery component manufacturing or assembly thresholds.[web:376][web:380]

**Evidence Themes**  
Policy, Incentives, Affordability, Battery Sourcing, Final Assembly Location, Eligibility Rules

**Primary Source**  
U.S. Treasury press release on proposed clean vehicle credit guidance and associated technical summaries of critical mineral and battery component requirements[web:376][web:382][web:380]

**Evidence Strength**

## SIG-POL-2023-002

**Date**  
2023-04-11

**Headline**  
EPA proposes strongest-ever light- and medium-duty vehicle emissions standards for model years 2027–2032

**Category**  
Policy

**Signal Class**  
Structural

**Signal Vector**  
Policy

**Signal Direction**  
Policy Change

**Effective Window**  
- Start: 2023-04-11 (proposal issued for public comment)  
- End: Proposal stage during this period (final rule anticipated later)[web:387][web:379][web:381][web:386]

**Observed Change**  
On April 11, 2023, EPA proposed new greenhouse gas and multi‑pollutant emissions standards for light‑ and medium‑duty vehicles for model years 2027 and later, projecting that the model year 2032 light‑duty standards would reduce fleet‑average greenhouse gas emissions by about 56% compared with the 2026 standards.[web:387][web:386] EPA analysis indicated that automaker compliance pathways under the proposal could result in electric vehicles comprising up to roughly 60–67% of new light‑duty vehicle sales by 2032.[web:387][web:375][web:381]

**Evidence Themes**  
Policy, Emissions Standards, Long-Term Targets, Regulatory Environment, Market Penetration

**Primary Source**
