[YEAR:2020] [SOURCE:EVForward 2020] [SECTION:1. Methodology]
Sample size: Total n=10,293 (Premium: n=1,649; Mainstream: n=8,135; Alternative Powertrain: n=509). Fieldwork: September - October 2019. Target population: U.S. market only, age 18-80 years old, must not work in market research, automotive, or for an electric utility company. Primary vehicle household must be MY2014 or newer, must have had an active role in the purchase/lease decision, and intend to purchase a new vehicle within 5 years. Survey methodology: 25-minute online survey. Weighting: Data weighted by segment, state, brand, age, gender, race & Hispanic. Methodology changes: None explicitly stated in the report.

[YEAR:2020] [SOURCE:EVForward 2020] [SECTION:EV Intention & Purchase Intent]
2% of new-vehicle buyers say their next vehicle will be a Battery Electric Vehicle (BEV) no matter what. 17% are waiting for a BEV that meets their needs. 10% believe a Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicle (PHEV) can work for them, even if a BEV may not. 45% have no interest in an EV for their next vehicle purchase. 26% answered "I don't know" regarding their next vehicle purchase. Overall purchase intent for a BEV is 29 on a 0-100 scale. 8% of consumers have a BEV Purchase Likelihood above 80 on a 0-100 scale. BEV Purchase Likelihood above 80 jumps to 37% if time to charge is 5 minutes. Assuming a $35,000 gasoline vehicle, respondents expect to pay an average of $38,119 for a battery electric version. Over 25% of respondents are likely to delay their next vehicle purchase until an EV that better meets their needs is available (14% very likely, 13% somewhat likely).

Weighting: Weighted by segment, powertrain, brand, state, age, gender, race & Hispanic. Methodology changes: Six additional brands were added to the brand consideration question in 2026. Extended-Range Electric Vehicle (EREV) was introduced to the study in 2026.

[YEAR:2026] [SOURCE:EVForward 2026] [SECTION:BEV Attitudes]
64% think BEVs are the future (-4 pts yoy). 56% would feel anxious if their next purchase had to be a BEV. 61% think new vehicle prices are at an all-time high. 41% think EVs are becoming even more expensive than gas vehicles. 62% think BEVs are not affordable. 56% think BEVs are more expensive to maintain than gas vehicles. 60% of non-BEV owners think a BEV will cause their home electricity bill to increase significantly. 64% think BEV batteries are unreliable need replacement during ownership. 75% who shopped for a vehicle in the past 6 months included at least one alternative powertrain (+4 pts yoy). 25% think PHEVs are here to stay (+4 pts yoy). 54% think EREVs are a great idea they would consider (+3 pts yoy).

[YEAR:2026] [SOURCE:EVForward 2026] [SECTION:Barriers]
Driving range is the strongest BEV barrier and is unchanged year over year, with roughly two in three saying it strongly deters them. Unknown reliability is a top-tier barrier. [YEAR:2026] [SOURCE:EVForward 2026] [SECTION:Market Evidence]
EV models: Production of the Dodge Charger EV was delayed. Honda 0 Saloon was discontinued prior to production. Ford discontinued the F-150 Lightning. GMC Hummer EV experienced delays. Tesla Model S and X discontinued. Battery electric trucks in the market include F-150 Lightning, Rivian R1T, Silverado EV, Slate, and Cybertruck. Government policy & Supply chain: Tariffs targeting Canada, Mexico, and China were imposed by the Trump administration. Incentives: The $7,500 federal EV tax credit was discontinued on September 30, 2025. 55% would be more likely to consider a BEV because of the $7,500 federal EV tax credit. 43% were unaware of the federal EV tax credit. Inflation & Fuel prices: Heading into the 2026 calendar year, there is growing economic uncertainty—high inflation and interest rates, spikes in gas prices, war in Iran. Charging Infrastructure: Reduced funding for public charging infrastructure.

[YEAR:2026] [SOURCE:EVForward 2026] [SECTION:Executive Observations]
"BEVs are now mainstream: over half of buyers know a BEV owner and familiarity is widespread". "Awareness ≠ conversion. In the past, BEV adoption relied heavily on awareness. Now that BEVs are more mainstream, momentum requires education and proven value". "Buyers evaluate BEVs through analytical reasoning rather than emotional impulse.". "Affordability is a considerable hurdle for the automotive industry and is amplified for BEVs.". "Misperceptions about BEV purchase prices heighten affordability barriers.". "Affordability concerns go beyond upfront purchase price and include associated costs and perceived financial risk, including battery replacement costs, expensive maintenance and repairs, home charger installation, and home and public charging costs". "The powertrain market is fragmenting.". "Public charging buildout alone is insufficient to drive adoption.". "Range anxiety is a proxy for a lack of confidence in the charging experience". "The Story Of 2026 Is Not BEV Rejection, It’s BEV Hesitation".

[YEAR:2026] [SOURCE:EVForward 2026] [SECTION:Strategic Recommendations]
"Educate buyers about the realities of owning a BEV, including the charging experience and associated ownership costs". "Make incentives impossible to miss.". "Approach BEV affordability as an ecosystem, not just the upfront purchase price. Educate shoppers about lifetime ownership costs and offer options to offset upfront costs". "Offer longer term financing and flexible lease options.". "Bundle a home charger and installation with the purchase of a BEV". "Offer battery warranties to reduce perceived risks about battery longevity and replacement". "Meet buyers where they are on their electrification journey and work from there.". "Treat PHEVs and EREVs as strategic adoption tools, not distractions.". "Reduce overreliance on range as a psychological safety net by making charging feel manageable, predictable, and convenient". "Take ownership of the entire charging experience, not just the vehicle. Partner with charge point operators to establish 'certified charging locations'". "Use audience segmentation to meet buyers where they are on their electrification journey and address barriers to adoption". What's New This Year COM-B Model: The COM-B (Capability, Opportunity, Motivation - Behavioral) framework was introduced to diagnose factors driving or preventing behavior change. EREV Inclusion: Extended-Range Electric Vehicles (EREV) were added as a specific powertrain category. Brand Consideration Additions: Six additional brands were added to the brand consideration question. "The Messy Middle": A new concept outlining the fragmented market caused by an expanding set of powertrain choices.
